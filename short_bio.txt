Dr. William Ratcliff received his BSE in Engineering Physics from the University of Michigan in 1997 and his Ph.D. in Physics from Rutgers university in 2003.  He is the recipient of a Department of Commerce Bronze medal (the highest recognition awarded by NIST) and is a Fellow of the American Physical Society. He is a physicist at NIST and an adjunct associate professor in the Materials Science and Engineering department at the University of Maryland, College Park. His research interests include topological materials, multiferroics, and the application of AI to neutron scattering. He is the founder of the Representational Analysis and Magnetic Structure School.
